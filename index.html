What this deck is

Software for car dealers: stock, workshop & books.

This deck is car-dealership software in plain language: stock on screen, workshop money per car (tyres, body, detailing / PPF), a proper expense book, and partner balances when money is pooled. For showrooms that run on WhatsApp today but need one ledger tomorrow. The application is a Windows program (.exe) on each PC. Cloud vs office is only where your database lives—on our servers or on your machine at the showroom. If the database stays at the office and you need access from outside, you use VPN into your network.

Plain problem

WhatsApp and Excel were never a ledger.

Picture Monday morning: Which car is still in paint or PPF? Who paid the tyre bill? How much cash is really left? Right now that lives in ten chats and three sheets—while customers expect the same story from sales, accounts, and the owner. This system puts each car, each bill, and each partner’s money in one place—so anyone you trust opens it and sees the same picture.

Owner view

The dashboard: “How’s the business?”

On this screen

Imagine a TV in the office: total value of the operation, money partners moved in or out, and simple charts—like “who holds how much of the pie” and how sales have been trending.

Why dealers care

You and your partners look at one headline before you argue. No one stays up merging Excel the night before a meeting.

Partner money

One file per investor

On this screen

Think partner card: name, phone, CNIC-style ID, how much they put in, any money taken out, date, who typed it, and whether they’re active.

Why dealers care

When someone asks “what’s my balance?” you open their card—not a voice note from six months ago. Fair bookkeeping for pooled deals.

New stock

Car arrives—open one form, done

On this screen

Drop in photos, set status (just bought, in prep…), then fill what you paid, who sold it to you, and any notes. One save when the keys land on the desk.

Why dealers care

That same record feeds the stock list, workshop jobs, and later the sale. No retyping the chassis story in three places.

Stock book

Every car on one list

On this screen

A table like a stock register: invoice no., who’s buying, which partner funded it, make/model/year, your asking price, and a colour tag—bought, in workshop, or sold.

Why dealers care

Sales and accounts stop shouting across the room. They point at the same row. Search and filters find a car in seconds when the customer is on the line.

Workshop bills

What you spent fixing each car

On this screen

A running job book: this Corolla—tyres at Pak Wheels; that Civic—denting at AutoMax; another line—PPF / detailing before handover. Each line says which car, what kind of work, which vendor, and a reference so you can match the bill.

Why dealers care

Before you fix a sale price you see real cost in the metal. Bookkeeping stays tied to the vehicle—not lost in a WhatsApp photo.

Goga Expenses

Enter a bill—same form every time

On this screen

The Goga Expenses entry screen: pick category and sub-category, short description, date, amount, who paid, and bank or cash. You can attach a receipt photo in the same flow so the proof sits with the line.

Why dealers care

Staff don’t invent their own columns. Every expense hits the book the same way—so the monthly view and your accountant stay aligned.

Goga Expenses

Month spend & reserves—live table

On this screen

The actual Goga Expenses view: tiles for this month’s total spend (and trend) plus reserves. Underneath, every line—car, work type, vendor, amount—so you see where the money went at a glance.

Why dealers care

Owner asks “what did we burn this month?”—the answer is on one screen, tied to real cars and vendors, not a half-finished spreadsheet.
